Character 1 · Lagos
Amaka
Market woman · Lagos
Amaka: "My name is Amaka. Every morning I wake up at 4am to set up my stall. Business is not easy — but we dey manage. Life get its own way of going. In January 2023, I fit still make small profit. That was life. Not perfect, but manageable."
Character 2 · Abuja
Emeka
Civil servant · Abuja
Emeka: "I am Emeka. Same salary, same job, every month. I used to think that was security. But what nobody tells you is this — a salary is only as powerful as what it can buy. And when prices move faster than your pay, you feel it. Quietly. Every single day."
Character 3 · Port Harcourt
Chidi
Business owner · Port Harcourt
Chidi: "I run a small provisions shop. Everything I sell, I have to move — and that means fuel. Always fuel. In 2023, petrol was ₦185. My delivery cost was manageable. My prices were competitive. My customers could afford what I sold. That was my world."

Inflation also peaked at 34.19% in 2024. Food prices climbed relentlessly. For millions of ordinary Nigerians — something quietly shifted.
Amaka's reality · 2024
Amaka: "Transport cost don go up. My suppliers increase price. I increase my price too — but my customers no get more money. So they buy less. My profit don shrink. I no dey expand again. I just dey... maintain."
Amaka: "Before, I was saving small small. Now I dey use my savings to survive. That is not the same thing."
Emeka's reality · 2024
Emeka: "My salary has not changed. But rent up. Food up. Transport up. School fees for my children — up. I now calculate everything. Every naira. Every single decision."
Emeka: "I used to live. Now I budget. There is a difference between the two — and I feel that difference every morning."
Chidi's reality · 2024
Chidi: "My delivery cost has tripled. To survive, I increased my prices. But my customers have less money too — so some stopped coming. I had to let my assistant go. Now I run everything alone."
Chidi: "I did not close my business. But the dream to grow it — to open a second shop — that dream is on hold. For now, I am just keeping the door open."
